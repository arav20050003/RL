Project Overview

THE RESEARCH PROBLEM & SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

Classical (s,Q) heuristics are inherently reactive. They manage baseline volatility gracefully but fail structurally during extended supply chain disruptions, completely blind to the semantic risk signals accelerating around them.

While Reinforcement Learning (RL) agents can technically learn adaptive policies, typical approaches rely purely on quantitative state embeddings, ignoring the qualitative news and information flow that human managers use to foresee shocks.

This project tests whether LLM-derived risk signals fed as continuous streams can improve resilience, avoiding the catastrophic over-hedging typical of simplistic binary disruption-aware RL extensions.

Variance Dominance

Episode profit std ≈ ±1,620 — variance entirely dominates mean differences. No policy demonstrates statistically robust dominance (all p > 0.25). The disruption behaves like a sledgehammer; either you get hit entirely or miss it completely across 100 episodes.

Stress Regime Saturation

All PPO variants converge to the identical max-throughput saturation policy (~-3385 profit). This is structurally highly rational under a 53% disruption rate. (s,Q) actually performs best here (−2,805) simply by blindly avoiding excessive reaction production costs.

How the LLM Pipeline Works

  1. Environment generates true_disruption_flag (0 or 1).
  2. NewsOracle samples from template library based on the true state.
  3. RiskParser applies keyword-weight scoring simulating semantic attention.
  4. Gaussian noise (σ=0.25) added to simulate LLM ambiguity → final risk_score ∈ [0,1]
